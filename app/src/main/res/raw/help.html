Doughlator help

Introduction

With doughlator you can create and quickly adjust their bread doughs and pastries. The application consists of three main views :

  • Recipe list view.
  • Recipe detail view.
  • Elaboration notes view.
Recipe list view

It is the main window recipes are listed, both those created by you and the recipes included in the application are listed.

Recipe detail view

In this view you can adjust the parameters of the recipe, add , edit, delete and send the recipe ingredients email .

From the menu options are available type of setting ( or ingredients by weight percentage adjustment ) and doubled the recipe.

Elaboration notes view

Notes view lets you view , edit and save processing steps and important points.

Recipe list

Add recipe

To add a recipe simply press the button on the lower right margin of the list . In the box that appears , type the name of the recipe and press the OK button. The recipe is automatically added to the list with the 4 basic ingredients ready to be edited .The box "Use as preferment " creates recipes based on preferments that may be incorporated in other recipes.

Delete recipe

Recipes are deleted from the list of recipes. Press for a while the recipe to be deleted . Since the confirmation box by pressing the OK button to accept .

Recipe detail

The detail view allows you to adjust the recipe simple and easy way . Moisturizing bar indicates at all times the rate of hydration of the mass in order to adjust said parameter at any time. The weight of the dough before baking to adjust amounts is also indicated. When editing ingredients it is convenient to classify them into three groups:

  • Reference ingredients (Flours).
  • Liquid ingredients.
  • Solid ingredients.

The ingredients used to reference correctly adjust the remaining ingredients of the recipe and calculate the rate of hydration. When creating a new recipe, always add the flour in the first position . This ingredient plus reference element is the base ingredient and is essential to adjust the reference remaining ingredients . You can not remove the base flour. Referenced ingredient considers all flour from cereal . Liquid ingredients directly influence the hydration of the dough. Liquid ingredients such as water, milk , oil , egg considered . The ingredients that are not referenced are considered solids or liquids . It is very important to consider this classification to properly prepare the recipe.

Add ingredients

The new ingredients are inserted from the "+ " button in the toolbar. Write the name , put the percentage or amount , according to the selected setting and marking the reference or liquid ingredient ( not mark anything as not falling under any of the two groups) . Pressing the OK button will be added to the list of ingredients . Adjusting the recipe is done automatically.

Edit ingredients

To edit an ingredient just click on it in the list of ingredients and the dialog box will appear . After making the desired changes , pressing the OK button to confirm the changes.

Remove ingredients

To remove an ingredient , keep it pressed until the dialog box appears . The other ingredients are recalculated by pressing the OK button.

Add preferment

You may insert a preferment (previously created) in a recipe. By incorporating preferment in the recipe, it will be inserted as if it were an ingredient and the amounts of flour and water will be subtracted to the recipe while maintaining the proportions. The other ingredients likely to have the preferment itself be taken into account to determine the final weight of the recipe. To do this, choose "add preferment option" from the menu and select the type and percentage/amount . The preferment appear in the list of ingredients and its rate of hydration in the upper right corner will indicate. If you want to change the preferment, you must perform the change in the recipe that defines the preferment and returns it to the recipe that uses it ( previously erasing the previous one)

Elaboration notes

Notes edition

From the elaboration notes view it is possible to write anything recipe such as: kneading, fermentation phases, times, temperatures, etc. To start to write or edit an existing note, click the pen icon that appears on the toolbar. To complete editing Press the disk icon in the toolbar.
